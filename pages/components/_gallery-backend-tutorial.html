Must Read Tips & Advice

Introduction: 4 Steps to Great Websites & Portfolios

The key is getting your portfolio and your story organized first - before creating your website and sharing your work. Doing things in the right order is the vital to success.

1. Create a great profile, which you use to tag art and exhibitions - (if you are a gallery or curator you can create multiple profiles for the artists you manage - see other tutorials)


2. Create an Art record- THEN - tag this record with images (allows you to switch which images you want to use for your artwork more easily)


3. Create Exhibitions - THEN add the Art in your portfolio to these exhibitions


4. Publish and share your Exhibitions of Art in a wide range of ways (apps, showrooms, websites and social media)

Key Buttons & Interactions

Exhibitions are the way you curate and showcase interconnected artworks that you want to share and tell a story about.

1. You must put art in an exhibition and PUBLISH it, before you can share it. CURATE!!


2. Make sure Art & Exhibitions are tagged with your name properly


3. SAVE BUTTON. Use the SAVE BUTTON often, so your changes stick.


4. Watch Your File Size - Struggling to upload an image. Try reducing its size to below 1MB

How Profiles Work - if you are an Artist

1. All Artworks need to be tagged with the profile of the Artist - YOU

2. Your Art can only be added to an Exhibition if YOU are tagged as the Artist in the Exhibition


3. Your CV in your App and Showroom is driven by your Profile information


4. On your vPatina driven website, Profile pages are also driven by your profile information

How Profiles Work - if you are a Curator/ Gallery

1. On your App and in the Showroom, your Profile is automatically attached to Exhibitions you create - giving you credit as a curator

2. You can also create and manage Profiles of the Artists you manage, so you can create artworks and exhibitons featuring them

Profiles - Creating & Using

Artists - Managing Your Profile

1. Manage your profile from the "Account/Settings" module

2. “Basic” & “Bio/ Photo” tabs are vital - choose a photo and write a bio that tells your story


3. Create a CV, keep it simple with free text, or add details about your can add your exhibtions, awards and press


4. Add “Selected Art”, and images, so these appear alongside your profile on your website, or when you share artwork or exhibitions via your app, social media or messaging (NB YOU NEED TO CREATE ARTWORKS FIRST BEFORE YOU CAN ADD THEM TO YOUR PORTFOLIO)


5. On the details tab, add your scoial media accounts - making it easier for people to follow you

Galleries & Curators - Managing Your Profile

The details tab includes profile information useful if you are a business

1. Add opening times - so people know when to come by and visit


2. Add multiple contact details - people can contact everyone in the team

Galleries & Curators - Creating & Managing Artist Profiles

If you represent multiple artists (e.g. if you are a gallery), you can create managed profiles so you can create art portfolios, exhibitions and web pages featuring these artists you represent

1. You will need to use the Artists/Contact section (if you can't see it you may need to get your account upgraded - which we can do for free - get in touch)

2. To create a managed profile use the green “Create artist” button

3. To edit a managed profile - click on the green edit icon next to the profile in the artists/contact table

4. To create artworks for your managed artist - tag the art record you create with the managed profile (see Art Tutorial section for more details on creating/editing/deleting artworks)

5. To create an exhibition featuring your managed artist - tag the exhibition with their managed profile (see Exhibition Tutorial for more instructions on creating/editing/deleting exhibitions)

6. If you are using vPatina to create your website - you can add artists profiles to certain web pages on your site

Artworks - Creating, Editing, Managing, Deleting

Adding a New Art Record

1. Manage your artworks from the "Artworks" module

2. Hit the green “Create artwork” button to create a new artwork


3. Then add minimum “Basic” information (title/ artist/ technique) to create a record


4. Then use the “Image” tab to add media to your art record


5. Finally, on the “Details” tab add as much, or as little, context as you wish, such as size, year, price, catergory...

Editing Existing Artworks

1. Click directly into the artwork table to edit the name, price, availability, dimensions


2. Click on the green edit icon in the action column - to open the artwork for editing

Searching Your Portfolio to Find a Specific Artwork

1. Enter key words into the search box to quickly narrow down your search of your porfolio


2. Use the drop down selector at the top of columns to filter art by type or status

Cloning Art - Quickly Creating a Series from One Artwork

1. In the “Actions” column of the artwork you want to clone, click on the orange “Clone” icon


2. When the pop-up launches, enter the name of the new artwork you are adding to your portfolio, and hit the green “Duplicate” button


3. If this new artwork has details that are different to the original (e.g. size, number of editions) most can be edited directly on the artwork table.


4. Click on the green edit button next to the new artwork in the action column, to add media to and edit details of the artwork

Downloading Art Records to Excel, CSV, etc.

1. The export function allows you to download all the artworks on the table when you hit the button, so the first step is to make sure the table displays the artworks you want to download.

(i) To download specific art records - check the art records you want to download, then choose the "Show" option on green "Selected" button

(ii) To download your entire portfolio, choose "All" from the rows per page selector

2. Hit the download records button and the choose the file type you want the records to be donwloaded as.

3. When your are prompted to choose a name for the file, make sure it has the correct file extension (for example ".xls" for Excel documents.

Deleting Artworks

1. Click on the check box next to the artwork or artworks you want to delete


2. Click on the green “Selected” button and then choose the “Delete” option


3. Hit the red "Delete" button to confirm your choice to permanently delete the selected art records

Exhibitions - Creating, Publishing, Sharing

Exhibitions - Create & Set Up

Exhibtions can be connected to real events witha a time and place (such as an art fair, gallery show or event) or they showcase a series of interconnected artworks you want to share and tell a story about (selections and showrooms)

1. Manage your exhibitions from the "Exhibitions" module

2. Hit the green “Create exhibition” button to begin creating your new exhibition

3. If your are an artists MAKE SURE the exhibition is tagged with your name (if you are a gallery/curator MAKE SURE the participating artists are tagged)


4. Choose the type of exhibition you are creating (Show/Fair/Selection/Showroom) give it a name and hit SAVE - after hitting SAVE you will be able to add art. REMEMBER TO HIT SAVE

5. Add additional details - Location, Dates, Events - (Vital if you want to use smart filters to build web pages)

Exhibitions - Add Artworks

1. On the "Art" tab in the exhibition, if you want to add art from your portfolion (the best way to share your art), hit the green “attach artworks” button NB YOU NEED TO SET UP THE ART IN YOUR PORTFOLIO FIRST.


2. Click on the check box next to the artwork or artworks you want to add to the exhibition

3. Click on the green “Selected” button and then choose the “Add to Exhibition” option

4. Trouble Shooting: If you can’t see the art you are looking for after hitting the “attach artworks” button it’s either because

(i) You haven’t addd art to your portfolio yet

(ii) You haven’t tagged yourself in the exhibition properly

Exhibitions - Add "Exhibition Images" to Support Artworks

To help tell your story, as well as curating art in an exhibition, you can add “Exhibition Images.”

These can be pictures of gallery interiors, openings, the inspiration for the artworks, or the artists’ studio, or a detail, or anything else you want.

1. As with artworks and exhibitions, you first need to create a record, so you can attach an image to it. So, on the “Art/Images" tab in the exhibition, hit the green “Create Exhibition Image Record” button to get started.


2. A pop up will launch - choose a type/tag for the image and add a caption, finally hit save, to create the “Exhibition Image” record.

3. The “Exhibition Image” details you entered will appear in the list of Art & Images in the exhibition. Now hit the “Add/Edit Media” button next to this record to attach a picture.

4. A media tab will open up. Use one of the green buttons to either upload an image or attach an image from your media drive.

Exhibitions - Select Title Image

On the “Title Image” tab you can select an image that will visually represent this exhibition on websites and showrooms. You have two choices.

(A) If you want to choose an artwork from the exhibition, then select the top radio button. This will open a drop-down menu that allows you to choose the artwork you want.


(B) If you want to use an image that isn’t an artwork, select the second “Upload/Attach” option. Two green buttons will appear that allow you to either upload an image or access the media drive to select an image to be the Title image for your exhibition.

Exhibitions - Publish & Share

1. On the Publish/Share Tab - turn the Exhibition on - so you can share it (via Showroom, App, or website).


2. The URL to the exhibition Showroom is a bit further down the page and you can cut and paste it to send the exhibition to your network via messaging and social media, or use the inbuilt social media functions


3. Clicking on the blue social media share icon in the Exhibiton Table action column, is also a shortcut to sharing that exhibtion

Exhibitions - Deleting

NB - When you delete an Exhibition you don’t delete the artworks in the exhibition - these remain in your portfolio (there are separate instructions on how to delete artworks).

1. Click on the check box next to the exhibition or exhibitions you want to delete

2. Click on the green “Selected” button and then choose the “Delete” option

3. Confirm your choice once prompted to permanently delete the selected Exhibition(s)

Media Drive - Upload, Organize, Attach

Media Drive - Overview & Purpose

The Media Drive is where every single image you upload to vPatina can be found, and it’s designed to do a few things:

1. Cloud Storage - Once uploaded to the drive, you have your media at your fingertips, anywhere you have access to a computer.

2. Organizing Media - Media can be tagged with keywords and also organized into folders, so you can categorize your media in as many ways as you want.

3. Managing Storage - Different vPatina plans have a limit on the number of images you can store, so use the Media Drive to delete images you no longer need.

4. Accessing & Using Media - Images in the media drive can be easily attached to Art, Exhibitions, Profiles and Website Logos.

TOP TIP: When accessing and using the media drive from an Exhibtion/Artwork/Profile, if you get stuck - use the red “Back” button to return to the Exhibition/Artwork/Profile you were at.

Media Drive - Adding Media

There are two ways to add media to your Media Drive and one place to find newly uploaded media.

1. Newly uploaded media is put in the “Uploads” folder.

2. To upload images while in the Media Drive, use the green “Upload Image” button to add media DIRECTLY to the “Uploads” folder...

this then takes you through to a panel where you can see all the media you have uploaded DIRECTLY to the media drives (as opposed to via Artworks/Exhibitions/Profile) - hit the green "Upload" button on this panel to upload more media files to the drive.

3. Whenever you use the green "Upload" button to add an image to a Profile, Artwork, Exhibition or Website Logo - the file is automatcally added to “Uploads” folder in the Drive

Media Drive - Folders

NOTE: Folders in vPatina are all on the same level, in other words, you can’t put a folder within a folder.

1. Use the “Create Folder” button to name and create a folder.

2. To enter a folder click on the folder name.

3. To get back to the root from within any folder - click on the back arrow.

4. To move media to a folder - check the box next to the media and use the green “Selected” button to choose the folder you want to move the media to.

Media Drive - Tags

Tags are key words added to folders and artworks, making it possible to have multiple categories for one piece of media (e.g. Picasso, 1946, Studio, Ceramics, Sculpture, Berlin).

This means you can find images quickly. You can use any tag you want, but, the tags also auto-complete on suggested tags, for your convenience.

1. When creating a new folder you can add Tags to it.

2. To add tags to an existing folder, use the Green Edit icon next to the folder...

then add, edit or delete the tags on the folder.

3. To add tags to a media, use the Green Edit icon next to the media.

then add, edit or delete the tags connectd to the file.

Media Drive - Deleting Media

1. Deleting Folders - check the box next to the Folders you want to delete...

and then use the green "Selected" button to delete the selected Folders.

NOTE: YOU CAN’T DELETE THE “UPLOADS” FOLDER

2. Deleting Media Files - check the box next to the media files you want to delete and then use the green Select button to delete the selected files.

3. Also possible, but not recommended (because it is not efficient) you can delete media after hitting the “Upload Image” button in the Media Drive - because this gives you access to all the media directly uploaded to your Media Drive (NOTE - this doesn’t included images uploaded through Art, Exhibitions, Profiles etc).

Media Drive - Attaching Files to Art, Exhibitions, Profiles, or Website Logo

1. When attaching images (to an Artwork, Exhibition, Profile or Website Logo), choose the green “Media Drive” button - which will take you to the media drive.

2. In the media drive, click on the folder in which the image you want is stored.

NOTE - if at this stage you click on the wrong folder accidentally - then use the red “Back” button to return to the Artwork/Exhibition/Profile you were at and try again - this time accessing the correct folder.

3. Inside the folder - if you need to - use the search function to find the image.

4. Check the box next to the medial file you want to attach.

5. Use the green Select button to attach the file to the artwork/exhibition/profile in question.

Apps - Installing, Showing & Sharing

App - Install it on Your Phone

Technical Note : Your App is a "Progressive Web App," which means you don't need to go to the "App" or "Play" store and we can give you a smooth experience on mobile and dektop (very well supported by google).

The easiest way to install the app on your phone is to first log into your vPatina account on your phone.

1. After logging in, click on your profile icon in the corner and choose "view profile" from the drop down menu

2. In your profile, click on the URL link on top of your image (this takes you to your public profile)

3. A soon as you get to this online public profile page - add it to your home screen as an icon

Alternatively, get the URL link by logging int your account on your PC:

1. Go to your account - where you can find the link to your online showroom profile

2. Then e-mail this link to your mobile (where you can bookmark it, as described above).

Showrooms - Create & Share

Showrooms - Create

Showrooms are exhibtions that are shared using our technology, so to create showroom simply follow the instructions for creating an Exhibition (see above).

TOP TIP - "Private Link" exhibtions are a great way to share art selectively with smaller circle of people - because these exhibitions don't appear on your website or on your profile. Users need the link to view them.

Share entire Showroom (inlcuding Private Links) from your account

1. Clicking on the blue share icon in the Exhibiton Table action column, it will take you to the tab that allows you to share that exhibtion

2. MAKE SURE THE EXHIBITION HAS BEEN PUBLISHED (IF IT'S OFF YOU CAN'T SHARE IT- SO TURN IT ON)

3. Use the social media share buttons to post the showroom to your social media feed - OR

4. Copy and paste the URL link above the exhibition into an e-mail or message or social media feed (NB THE URL IS THE ONLY WAY TO SHARE THE PRIVATE LINKS)

Share Showrooms & Art within a Showroom, from App

1. In the App, Navigate to the showroom in and share using the social media buttons - OR

2. If you want to emphasize a particular artwork in a showroom, navigate to that artwork and use the social media buttons to share - OR

3. While browsing in full screen, use the share function

Share Art within a Showroom from vPatina Website

1. Go to your website (if you have created one using our website builder), go full screen on an artwork and then use the share function
